About Me

I am currently working as a postdoctoral fellow in the Theoretical Biophysics Group at Boston University, comprised by Professors Pankaj Mehta, Kirill Korolev and Daniel Segrè, with their students and postdocs. I obtained my PhD in Physics from MIT in 2017, where I studied nonequilibrium statistical mechanics with Professor Jeremy England. Before starting my doctoral work, I obtained a Master of Studies in Philosophy of Physics from Oxford University, where I did some work on foundations of classical thermodynamics with Professor Harvey Brown. When I’m not doing physics, I enjoy studying philosophy, particularly metaphysics and philosophy of science. I also like to spend time contemplating the beauty of nature directly, whether hiking Mount Lafayette or strolling along the Charles River Esplanade. For more information, download my current CV.
